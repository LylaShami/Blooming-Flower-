Menstruel Disc


Menstrual discs are simialr to menstrual cups as they are both inserted into the vagina and collects blood. The difference between a menstrual cup and a menstrual disc is that a menstrual cup is placed below your cervix and extends into your canal while a menstraul disc fits back into your vagina fornix. Both a menstrual disc and a menstrual cup have similar pros and cons. You can refer to the link below to learn how to use a menstrual disc. Menstrual discs are usually more messy than a menstrual cup. A menstrual disc should usually be worn up to 12 hours but this also depends on how heavy your flow is. Compared to menstrual cups, there have not been much serioous risks like TSS (toxic shock syndrome). However, you can learn more about TSS in the link below. It is important to note that most, but not all, menstrual discs are not meant to be reused. This also means that they are not the most environemental friendly period product to use.
