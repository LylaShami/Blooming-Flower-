Menstruel Cups


If you are new to using menstrual cups you should first talk to a gynecologist. Before buying a menstrual cups it is good to know what size will fit you. You will need take agem lenght of your cervix, whether or not you have heavy flow, the cup capacity and many more. Before you put in your menstrual cup you should make sure that you have washed your hands thoroughly adn that your menstrual cup is clean in order to prevent unwanted bacteria. Most people use menstrual cups because it is environmental friendly unlike pads and tampons. Some pros to menstrual cups are that they are affordable, they are safer than tampons adn they hold mroe blood than Both tampons and pads. Some cons to menstrual cups is that they can be messy, they can be hard to insert, and it mgiht be tough to find One that fits you. Like tampons, menstrual cups can also put you at risk for TSS (toxic shock syndrome). To learn more information about TSS, please refer to the link below or talk to a health professional.
