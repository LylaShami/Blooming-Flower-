Our Mission

Who Are We? Why? Tools

Growing up, we found the education about periods and our reproductive health was subpar. There are many stigmas surrounding women’s health, which can create significant barriers for women seeking medical care or accessing health resources. Despite being a natural and necessary bodily function, menstruation is often stigmatized and shrouded in shame and secrecy. This can lead to women feeling embarrassed or ashamed about their periods and may prevent them from seeking the care and resources they need. It is our goal to provide you with a quick and easy way to learn about menstruation, without worrying about doing the research and figuring-it-out yourself.

Understanding women’s bodies: By learning about periods, you can better understand menstrual cycles, including when a person is likely to ovulate, when the period is due, and the symptoms or changes during each phase of the menstrual cycle.

Promoting reproductive health: Knowing about periods can also help promote reproductive health, as it can help individuals identify potential issues or irregularities that may need to be addressed with a healthcare provider.

Empowering yourself and others: By educating yourself and others about periods, you can help break down stigma and promote a more open and supportive environment around reproductive health.

Planning and preparation: Understanding a menstrual cycle can help plan and prepare for periods, including purchasing necessary supplies and scheduling activities or events around your cycle.

Advocating for change: Lack of access to menstrual products and education around periods is a global issue. Knowing about periods and advocating for change can help ensure that all individuals have access to the resources and education they need to manage their menstrual cycles safely and effectively.
